# BALA Medical Safety Rules

BALA is a health-awareness app for personal use. It is not a medical device.

## Prohibited claims

BALA must never claim or imply that it can:

- Predict heart attacks or cardiac arrest
- Prevent cardiac arrest or any medical event
- Diagnose any disease or condition
- Detect early warning signs of disease
- Treat medical conditions
- Replace a doctor or healthcare professional
- Act as emergency monitoring or an emergency service

## Safe wording

Use these terms instead:

- guide
- signals
- awareness
- recovery
- balance
- check-in
- care
- listen to your body
- daily health companion
- personal awareness
- daily signals
- patterns
- baseline
- recent trend
- talk to a healthcare professional if concerned

## Emergency boundary

- Urgent symptoms (chest pain, shortness of breath, fainting) cap the score and
  show "seek urgent help" copy.
- BALA never claims to detect emergencies. It reminds the user to seek help.

## Doctor-ready summary boundaries

- The doctor-ready timeline is a plain-text summary of recent check-ins.
- It is framed as something "to share with your healthcare professional."
- It does not contain scores, diagnoses, or medical interpretations.
- It is copyable and downloadable as .txt only.

## Tone

- Calm, respectful, non-scary.
- Never shame the user for low sleep, low activity, symptoms, or recovery dips.
- No fear-based messaging.
