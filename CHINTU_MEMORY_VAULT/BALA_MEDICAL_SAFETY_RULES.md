# BALA Medical Safety Rules

BALA is a personal **health-awareness** app. It is **not a medical device**, not a
diagnostic tool, and not an emergency service. These rules are non-negotiable and
override any product, copy, or marketing impulse.

## Prohibited claims

BALA must never claim or imply that it can:

- Predict, detect, or warn about heart attacks or cardiac arrest.
- Prevent cardiac arrest or any medical event.
- Diagnose any disease, condition, or syndrome.
- Detect early warning signs of disease.
- Treat any medical condition.
- Replace a doctor, nurse, or healthcare professional.
- Act as emergency monitoring or an emergency service.
- Triage symptoms.
- Be FDA / EMA / CE-marked, certified, validated, or cleared.

This applies to in-app copy, marketing copy, tooltips, voice replies, push
copy, screenshots, and anything generated by Chintu / Claude / Codex / ChatGPT.

## Safe wording

Use the following words and phrases instead:

- awareness
- guide
- signals
- body signals
- recovery
- balance
- check-in
- care
- listen to your body
- daily health companion
- personal awareness
- daily signals
- patterns
- baseline
- recent trend
- "talk to a healthcare professional if you are concerned"

Voice-over and notification copy must follow the same vocabulary.

## Safe father story (the only public version)

BALA is named in memory of the founder's father, Balaji. The **only** version of
this story that may appear in any public-facing copy, screenshots, or interviews
is:

> I built BALA in memory of my father, Balaji. His name inspired this app.
> BALA is my attempt to help people listen to their body signals earlier and
> take small steps toward better health awareness.

Do **not** state, hint at, or speculate about cause of death, medical timeline,
specific conditions, hospitals, or any other private detail in public copy.

## Doctor / emergency boundary

- Urgent symptoms (chest pain, shortness of breath, fainting) cap the BALA Score
  and surface "seek urgent help" guidance.
- BALA never claims to detect emergencies; it only reminds the user to seek help.
- BALA never lists a hotline number it has not verified for the user's region.
- BALA never suggests delaying medical care.
- The "Today's Guide" is rule-based, deterministic, and clearly framed as guidance
  for awareness, not medical advice.

## Doctor-ready summary boundaries

- The doctor-ready timeline is a plain-text summary of recent check-ins.
- It is framed as something "to share with your healthcare professional."
- It contains no scores, no diagnoses, no interpretations.
- It is copyable and downloadable as `.txt` only.
- It never leaves the device on its own; the user must choose to share the file.

## Copy review rules

Before any code/doc commit:

1. Read every changed user-facing string.
2. Reject any string that uses a prohibited word (predict / detect / prevent /
   diagnose / treat / cure / emergency / monitor / replace doctor / clinical /
   medical-grade / early warning).
3. Reject any string that names a disease or condition unless the user typed it.
4. Reject any string that promises an outcome ("we will save your life", "this
   will lower your blood pressure", etc.).
5. Reject any string that shames the user for low sleep, low activity, recovery
   dips, or symptoms.
6. Reject fear-based push copy ("you may be at risk").
7. Confirm the copy uses safe wording above.
8. Confirm the privacy promise is unchanged ("everything stays on your device").

If a string is borderline, soften it. When in doubt, prefer awareness language
over advice language.

## Tone

- Calm, respectful, honest.
- No hype, no fear, no urgency theatre.
- Treat the user as an adult who knows their own body.
- Never shame.
